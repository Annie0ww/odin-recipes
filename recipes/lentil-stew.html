French lentil stew

Description

Lentil stew is an easy to make one pot dish that can make many servings. I make it to feed the fam for a few days. You can als make it one day in advance because every day the taste will get stronger. Perfect for those rainy days or lazy days, where you don't want to stand in the kitchen for long. All the ingredients could also work for a slow cooker, just let the cooker work for at least 5 hours to thoroughly cook the lentils

ingredients

  • 200 gram Dry green and yellow lentils
  • 200 grams brown lentils from can or jar
  • Sausages or bacon strips for meat
  • Olive oil or any preferred oil for cooking
  • Green part of leek, you could add a white onion (I unfortunately have an allergy to the alloy family so do not add onion or garlic, but feel free to add these in)
  • 3 carrots
  • white wine vinegar
  • tinned diced tomatoes
  • any beans of you liking, i add a tin of cannelloni beans
  • For seasoning, a stock cube, 2 bayleaf, a pinch of bean herbs, a pinch of mint, a pinch of marjorie and a pinch of ground sage.
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • (Opstional) fresh parsley and cheese for garnish

Steps

Step 1
Soak your dry lentils for atleast 5 hours, or overnight in the fridge
Step 2
Dice your vegetable and heat up oil in a pan big enough to fit all your ingredients
Step 3
Slice your sausages in rounds and your bacon in small pieces and fry in the pan till browned
Step 4
Add you diced vegetables and stir thoroughly
Step 5
Add a dash of vinegar to loosen up the crunchy bits on the bottom of the pan
Step 6
Add the diced tomatoes, beans and enough water to soak the content of the pan. Add a stock cube and the rest of the seasonings, salt and pepper to taste.
Step 7
Mix all of it thoroughly together and let simmer for 1 hour on low to medium heat. Do not let it violently boil, and stir occasionally
Step 8
Serve on a plate or fitting bowl, you could add some cheese and fresh parsley for garnish. Enjoy!
